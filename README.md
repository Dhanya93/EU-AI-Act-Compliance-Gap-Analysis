# 🇪🇺 EU AI Act — Compliance Gap Analysis

> A GRC learning and portfolio project demonstrating a structured compliance gap analysis of three AI systems against the EU AI Act (Regulation EU 2024/1689), including risk tier classification, obligation mapping, and remediation planning.

---

## 📌 Important — Learner Context

This gap analysis was created as a **learning and portfolio project** to demonstrate understanding of the EU AI Act structure, risk tier classification, and compliance obligations. It is based on the official published text of the EU AI Act and publicly available compliance guidance.

Article references are included to show framework literacy — in practice, detailed legal interpretation and compliance implementation would involve qualified legal counsel and certified AI governance professionals.

> This project demonstrates: EU AI Act risk tier classification | Identification of applicable obligations per tier | Structured gap analysis methodology | Remediation planning thinking

## 🔴 Key Findings

### AI-001 — Internal LLM Chatbot (Limited Risk)
- Art.50.1 — AI disclosure not sufficiently prominent at session start
- Art.50.4 — No AI-generated content labelling mechanism
- **Overall: Low regulatory risk — 2 medium gaps**

### AI-002 — HR Screening AI (High Risk) ⚠️ CRITICAL
- Art.9 — No AI risk management system
- Art.10 — No training data governance or bias analysis
- Art.11 — No technical documentation
- Art.12 — No automated logging of AI decisions
- Art.43 — No conformity assessment completed
- Art.71 — Not registered in EU database
- **Overall: 6 non-compliant items — must resolve before August 2026**

### AI-003 — Fraud Detection Model (High Risk) ⚠️ SIGNIFICANT GAPS
- Art.43 — No conformity assessment completed
- Art.49 — No declaration of conformity
- Art.71 — Not registered in EU database
- Art.9/10/11 — Partial compliance with documentation and governance
- **Overall: 3 non-compliant, 6 partial — August 2026 deadline at risk**
